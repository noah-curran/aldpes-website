Why is the name of the organization so long?

Phi Eta Sigma was founded as a freshman honor society designed to promote academic achievement among male students. Alpha Lambda Delta was originally founded as an honor society strictly for girls at the University of Illinois in 1924. A chapter was founded at Purdue University in 1926. In 1979, Alpha Lambda Delta became a co-ed national honor society. Each of these societies operates separately at the national level. However, both Alpha Lambda Delta and Phi Eta Sigma operate as one entity on the Purdue University campus, as well as many other campuses across the nation.

Is this a legitimate national honor society? There are so many national honor societies to choose from.

We can assure you that Alpha Lambda Delta Phi Eta Sigma is legitimate. It has over seventy-five years of rich history at hundreds of universities across the country. Only the brightest students are offered membership each year. In addition, the Alpha Lambda Delta and Phi Eta Sigma National Honor Societies are both long-standing members of the Association of College Honor Societies.

What are the membership requirements?

First of all, you must be a first-year student at the university. Secondly, you need to earn a 3.50 GPA during your first or second semester in attendance.

I transferred to Purdue from a college that did not have an Alpha Lambda Delta Phi Eta Sigma Chapter. Am I eligible for membership?

Freshman transfer students from institutions that do not have an Alpha Lambda Delta Phi Eta Sigma chapter, to be eligible for membership, must have the required Alpha Lambda Delta Phi Eta Sigma grade point average on the courses taken during the first full circular period in the institution to which they transfer. In addition, the cumulative average of the grades of the student's transfer credits combined with the grades in the institution in which the student is currently enrolled must equal the Alpha Lambda Delta Phi Eta Sigma academic grade requirement

What if my GPA drops below a 3.50 after I become a member of Alpha Lambda Delta Phi Eta Sigma?

While we do encourage members to maintain academic excellence throughout their time at Purdue, students are not required to maintain their GPA after they have been initiated. After you have been inducted into Alpha Lambda Delta Phi Eta Sigma you are a lifetime member.

How much do I need to pay in membership dues?

Membership dues are $80.00. This payment qualifies you as a member of both the Alpha Lambda Delta and Phi Eta Sigma National Honor Societies and as a member of the Purdue chapter of Alpha Lambda Delta Phi Eta Sigma.

Breakdown:

Alpha Lambda Delta national dues are $25

Phi Eta Sigma dues are $20

Local dues are $35

Total: $80

This one time payment qualifies you as a lifetime member of both honor societies.

If I join Alpha Lambda Delta Phi Eta Sigma as a lifetime member, will I be ineligible to join other national honor societies?

No, you are certainly free to join other honor societies. You are allowed to be a member of Alpha Lambda Delta Phi Eta Sigma and a member of other honor societies.

Is there a pledge week similar to the initiation into the Greek system?

No, there is not a pledge week or initiation requirement. As long as you meet the two membership requirements, you are virtually a member. There is an initiation ceremony for you and your parents. There is an optional banquet that follows the initiation ceremony, as well.

As a member of Alpha Lambda Delta Phi Eta Sigma am I required to participate in service projects and chapter events?

We do encourage our members to become involved in the various service projects and activities of the chapter, but we do not require attendance. You will be regularly informed of scheduled events for members. We truly hope you will take advantage of these exciting opportunities.

After I become a member, how do I get involved?

We will inform you of any events or important news through email, our newsletter, and this website. Please feel free to contact our officers via email with any questions you may have. We will certainly be glad to help you in any way we can. We are very excited to talk with members who wish to actively participate in the local chapter of Alpha Lambda Delta Phi Eta Sigma.

Are there any leadership opportunities?

Yes, there are many leadership positions within the executive council of the Purdue Chapter. This body is made up entirely of sophomore representatives. A new council is selected each spring, usually in April. Details regarding the selection and interview process will be made available as April draws nearer.

What should I wear for initiation?

The dress code for initiation is the standard "business professional" shirts and ties for men, and the equivalent for women. Most people will be wearing suits, skirts, and dresses.

Jo Anne J. Trow Undergraduate Scholarship

The National Council offers 36 Jo Anne J. Trow Undergraduate Scholarships annually, ranging from $1,000 to $6,000 each. There are five at $6,000, five at $3,000, five at $2,000, and 21 at $1,000 each. Sophomores who were initiated into Alpha Lambda Delta between January 1 and December 31, 2017 and who have maintained a 3.5 cumulative grade point average are eligible to apply.

The application can be found online at: http://www.nationalald.org/trow/

Purdue can submit three applications to the national chapter for consideration. COMPLETE Applications must be submitted online NO LATER than Monday, March 26, 2018. This will give the Purdue chapter time to name the three finalists for national competition by the National deadline of Monday, April 2, 2018.

If you have questions, please email chapter advisor Dr. Susan Huffman at smhuffman@purdue.edu.

The $10,000 Clark Award is presented annually to the member who, in the judgment of our Scholarship Committee, best exemplifies the ideals of Phi Eta Sigma and is selected from among the entire pool of scholarship applicants, both graduate and undergraduate.

Phi Eta Sigma Graduate & Undergraduate Scholarships

Scholarships range in value from $1,000 to $5,000 for undergraduates and up to $7,000 for graduate students. Both graduate and undergraduate students witha 3.5 GPA are eligible to apply for this scholarship. The same form is used for both graduate and undergraduate (Sophomore & Junior) applicants.

Go to https://www.phietasigma.org/how_to_apply for more instructions and to access the application AFTER February 1, 2018.

Applicants for the graduate scholarship may submit their COMPLETE application online directly to the national office NO LATER than March 31, 2018.

Undergraduate applicants must submit their COMPLETE application online to the national office NO LATER than Monday March 26 so that the local chapter can select their top three finalists for the national competition and have them submitted by the national office deadline of March 31, 2018.

For questions, please contact advisor Dr. Susan Huffman at smhuffman@purdue.edu
